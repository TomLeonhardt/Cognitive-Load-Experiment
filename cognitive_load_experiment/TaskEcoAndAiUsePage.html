Prisoner's Dilemma

Imagine two competing companies, Company A and Company B, are considering whether or not to invest in advertising.

Both companies know that the decision of the other company will affect their own profits.

Here are the details:

If both companies invest in advertising, each company earns a profit of 4 million euros.

If Company A invests in advertising and Company B does not, Company A earns a profit of 10 million euros and Company B earns a profit of 1 million euros.

If Company B invests in advertising and Company A does not, Company B earns a profit of 10 million euros and Company A earns a profit of 1 million euros.

If neither company invests in advertising, each company earns a profit of 6 million euros.

The companies must simultaneously decide whether to invest in advertising or not. Their decisions are summarized in the following payoff matrix:

Company B: Invest Company B: Do not invest
Company A: Invest (4, 4) (10, 1)
Company A: Do not invest (1, 10) (6, 6)

Task: Analyze the payoff matrix and determine the Nash equilibrium for this game.

Based on your analysis, which strategy would you choose as the manager of Company A?

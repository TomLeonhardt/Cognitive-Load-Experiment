What is Cognitive Load Theory?

Cognitive Load Theory explains how our cognitive resources are used when performing tasks. It includes three main components:

  • Intrinsic Load: The natural difficulty of the task itself.
  • Extraneous Load: The mental load caused by how the information is presented.
  • Germane Load: The mental effort used to process information and build knowledge.

High cognitive load can weaken decision-making processes, leading to errors and suboptimal choices.

Role of AI in Reducing Cognitive Load

AI can assist in reducing cognitive load by handling complex problems, providing recommendations, and automating routine tasks. This can help individuals make better decisions by offloading some of the cognitive burden.
